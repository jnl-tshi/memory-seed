### Decision

- D: User reported that the within-lane color cycling (each lane cycles through a family of shades
  so daisy-chained branches sharing a lane read as distinct) still looked too similar - two shades
  of the same hue read as "basically the same line" at a glance. Reworked each family's four shades
  to differ by hue, not just lightness/tint: e.g. the green family now runs true-green / grass-green
  / olive-yellow-green / emerald-teal instead of four light-to-dark greens. All shades stay at
  medium saturation/lightness so they remain legible against both the light and dark theme
  backgrounds (`--bg: #0f1115` dark, `#f6f7fb` light) - avoided near-black or near-white members
  that would wash out on one theme.
- R: Lightness-only variation (the previous families) collapses under casual viewing because hue is
  the strongest perceptual differentiator; humans distinguish hues faster than shades of one hue.
  Rotating hue within the family preserves "this lane is the green lane" while making consecutive
  entries pop.
