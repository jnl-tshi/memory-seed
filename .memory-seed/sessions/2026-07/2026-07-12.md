## 2026-07-12 11:40 - Fix: more visually distinct hues within Trail lane color families

```yaml

```

### Decision

- D: User reported that the within-lane color cycling (each lane cycles through a family of shades
  so daisy-chained branches sharing a lane read as distinct) still looked too similar - two shades
  of the same hue read as "basically the same line" at a glance. Reworked each family's four shades
  to differ by hue, not just lightness/tint: e.g. the green family now runs true-green / grass-green
  / olive-yellow-green / emerald-teal instead of four light-to-dark greens. All shades stay at
  medium saturation/lightness so they remain legible against both the light and dark theme
  backgrounds (`--bg: #0f1115` dark, `#f6f7fb` light) - avoided near-black or near-white members
  that would wash out on one theme.
- R: Lightness-only variation (the previous families) collapses under casual viewing because hue is
  the strongest perceptual differentiator; humans distinguish hues faster than shades of one hue.
  Rotating hue within the family preserves "this lane is the green lane" while making consecutive
  entries pop.
- F: `memory-trace/memory_trace/static/app.js`, `memory-trace/memory_trace/static/index.html`
  (cache-bust tag bump, per existing convention).
- T: Live-verified via the exposed `trailModel()` against the real corpus and a screenshot - lane 1
  cycles `#3fa66a` -> `#16a34a` -> `#7cb342` -> `#0f9b8e` for consecutive branches, visibly distinct.
  `memory-trace` test suite: 63 passed.

### Decision

- D: Two Trail changes at user direction. (1) Palette: kept the cycling-pack design but reverted to
  the brighter, higher-saturation older colors (the muted Okabe-Ito/ColorBrewer triads read as too
  dim), and reduced to four packs of three (`6f7cff/3fa66a/d9941a`, `d94b63/22b8cf/7cb342`,

  instead of cycling, so depth stays calm and never masquerades as a lively primary lane.
  (2) Indent: each row's time+title now indents per-row to follow the git-graph silhouette - text
  starts just right of the rightmost lane alive at that row (fork-to-merge occupancy envelope), so
  it hugs the lanes (wide where many branches run, sliding left toward main where only the trunk
  remains) instead of sitting at a fixed column past the widest point.
- R: User: old colors were "brighter and more readable"; liked the unique-pack cycling pattern; and
  wanted the "indentation of the times and entries [to] follow the lanes" (annotated contour). The
  envelope (not each entry's own lane) is both what the drawn contour shows and what's required so
  text never crosses a parallel lane.
- A: Implemented indent via an overlay: the SVG rail became an inert full-height background and the
  rows float over it, each carrying a `--indent` CSS var. Row hover/selection/pinned highlights and
  the chain bracket were re-anchored to `--indent` (gradient starts at the text) so they never paint
  over the lanes showing through beside the text.
- F: `memory-trace/memory_trace/static/app.js` (palette, `envelopeLane`/`rowIndent`, per-row
  `--indent`), `memory-trace/memory_trace/static/styles.css` (overlay layout, indent-anchored
