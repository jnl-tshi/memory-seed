### Decision

- D: User annotated a live Trail screenshot showing two green lines running in parallel and asked
  that every parallel-or-adjacent branch get a unique color. Root cause in `trailModel()`
  (`memory_trace/static/app.js`): color was assigned by `order`, each branch's position in a
  global shortest-lived-first sort, wrapping every 10 branches (`trailBranchColors.length`) with
  no relation to `lane`. Confirmed live against the real 334-entry corpus before fixing: `main`
  (lane 0) and `claude-feature-recording-tooling` (lane 2) both rendered `#6f7cff`, and

  (lane 3) both rendered `#3fa66a` - simultaneously visible, same color, exactly the reported bug.
  Fix: key `colorOf` on `lane` instead of `order`. Lanes are already guaranteed collision-free for
  anything parallel or adjacent (`laneIntervals` never lets overlapping spans share one), so
  keying color to the same axis makes "no two visible lines share a color" an invariant rather
  than a coincidence of sort order. Branches that reuse a freed lane inherit its color too, which
  is fine - they never appear on screen at the same time.
