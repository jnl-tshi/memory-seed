### Summary

- Decision diagrams are clickable in the React client (badge or reader figure -> zoom/pan modal),
  `esr` reports diagram coverage, and the three sidecars owed for 2026-07-21 are written.

### Decisions

#### D1 - Report diagram coverage as a count, never as a verdict

- D: `esr` gains a "Decision diagrams" section: how many of the session's entries carry a sidecar,
  and the date of the last sidecar anywhere. No opinion on whether any given entry needed one.
- R: JNL noticed a full day with no diagrams. Measuring showed it was not one bad day: 12 diagrams on
  2026-07-16 (the day the feature was built), then **zero across five days and 131 entries**, with the
  explain-the-skip half peaking at 5-of-20 on 07-15 and fading too. So the convention never became
  load-bearing, and nothing anywhere reported that.
  I first built the obvious thing - a keyword heuristic over the skill's own positive-trigger list -
  and measured it before shipping: it flagged **35% of all entries**, matching "worktree" in a passing
  validation line and "topology" in a feature name. A check that fires on one entry in three teaches
  its reader to skip it. It was dropped.
- A: The deciding argument was not the measurement but this module's own docstring - "Judgment stays
  with the agent - this reports, it never fixes." A trigger heuristic would have `esr` making the
  authoring judgement, which is the one thing it promises not to do. The bare fact is enough: the
  lapse is only visible as a RUN of empty days, which no single day's view shows, and that is exactly
  what "last sidecar anywhere" surfaces.

#### D2 - Clickable diagrams in React, reusing the vanilla class names

- D: A diamond badge in the Trail, or a diagram figure in the reader, opens a modal viewer with
  zoom/pan, Fit, Escape-to-close and backdrop-to-close. Zoom maths lives in `diagramZoom.ts` as pure
  functions; the class names and geometry are lifted verbatim from the vanilla stylesheet.
- R: JNL asked for parity with the vanilla JS. The React badge was an inert `<span>` whose tooltip
  said "open the entry to view" - and opening the entry only rendered the diagram inline in the
  inspector column, which is narrower than most authored diagrams. So a wide diagram was effectively
  unreadable in the React client with no way to enlarge it. Reusing vanilla's class names rather than
  inventing new ones means one stylesheet change tracks both UIs instead of them drifting.
- A: The badge fetches the entry's own chunk rather than requiring selection first, because the point
  of a preview badge is to look WITHOUT navigating away from what you are reading; it reuses the
  loaded chunk when it is already the selected entry. Zoom is extracted to a pure module for the same
  reason as graphLayout/graphEdges: the arithmetic (clamping, fit-scale, zoom-about-cursor) is where
  the bugs are and none of it needs a DOM.

#### D3 - Diagnosed the wrong cause, and was corrected by the user mid-turn

- D: The viewer's bad first render was a MISSING CSS RULE - `.diagram-stage .diagram-svg { width:
  100%; height: auto; }`, which I dropped when porting the vanilla styles. Restored.
- R: Seeing the modal render nodes strung out in a wide unreadable row, I concluded the diagrams
  themselves were at fault: `arc2d` has genuinely zero `subgraph` support (verified), and my sidecars
  used the tier-subgraph layout the `compact_mermaid_diagrams` skill prescribes, so "the house style
  is unsupported by the renderer" was a tidy story. JNL said plainly that the inspector renders the
  diagrams correctly - the same diagrams, the same renderer - which falsified it in one line. The
  difference was never the source; it was that the inspector's inline `.diagram-svg` sizes with
  `max-width` plus `overflow-x: auto`, while the viewer's stage needs `width: 100%` because the stage
  is what scales.
- A: I had already rewritten the three diagrams before the correction. They are kept, because the
  subgraph finding IS true and independently worth acting on - `arc2d` cannot draw the construct the
  skill tells authors to use, so a subgraph diagram silently loses its grouping in both UIs. That is a
  live contradiction between `compact_mermaid_diagrams.md` and the renderer, recorded here and NOT
  fixed in this entry.
  The process lesson is the recurring one for the day: a plausible story built from one observation,
  corrected by someone who checked a second surface. The cheap test I skipped was comparing the
  inspector and the viewer on the same diagram before theorising.

### Summary

  parses, resolving the skill-vs-renderer contradiction filed as a follow-up earlier today. The
  measured subset is narrower, and the failure mode uglier, than the follow-up assumed.

### Decisions

#### D1 - Narrow the skill to the measured subset instead of teaching the renderer `subgraph`

- D: The skill now documents the verified Arc 2d subset — `flowchart`/`graph`/`sequenceDiagram`
  headers, `[]`/`()`/`{}` nodes, `-->` and `-->|label|` edges, `<br/>` label breaks — and forbids
  `subgraph` in sidecars. The renderer is unchanged.
- R: JNL's call, and the measurement supports it. I ran every candidate form through `parseDiagram`
  rather than reading the Mermaid docs, and the subset is small enough that documenting it is a page,
  while widening the renderer to match the docs would mean containers, nested layout and a second
  layout pass in two hand-written twins that currently agree line for line.
- A: The follow-up said grouping is "silently dropped". It is not silent. `subgraph`, `direction` and
  `end` each satisfy the node regex, so they render as **stray rectangles labelled with those
  keywords**: the skill's own six-line tier example produces five boxes, three of them syntax noise.
  I would have written the weaker claim if I had trusted the handoff instead of running it. That
  changed the framing of the whole document — this is a corruption warning, not a degradation note.
- A: Two other findings reshaped the guidance. Every non-`-->` arrow (`~~~`, `---`, `-.->`, `==>`)
  fails to parse as an edge AND **drops its right-hand node from the diagram**, so the skill's old
  rules 2 and 5 — "use invisible `~~~` links to force a grid" — were actively deleting nodes, not
  merely failing to align them. And `flowchart BT` is not honoured: only `LR`/`RL` change the axis,
  so `BT` silently renders top-down. Rule 5 was outside the brief's "replace rules 1-3" but had to go
  for the same reason.
- F: `.memory-seed/skills/compact_mermaid_diagrams.md` and its seed twin (byte-identical, md5
  `912111e8`). The four new Mermaid examples were themselves run through `parseDiagram`: each yields
  exactly its intended nodes and edges with no stray boxes.

#### D2 - Scope the prohibition to sidecars, not to Mermaid everywhere

- D: The ban on `subgraph` applies to `.memory-seed/sessions/diagrams/**` only. Authored Markdown
  under `docs/**` keeps full Mermaid, grouping included.
- R: Checked before asserting: there is no `mermaid` dependency anywhere in `memory-trace`, and the
  only `renderDiagramBlock`/`parseDiagram` call sites render diagram sidecars. So Arc 2d's reach ends
  at the sidecars; `docs/**` diagrams are rendered by whatever full Mermaid engine the reader has,
  where `subgraph` is ordinary valid syntax. A blanket ban would have degraded the docs for nothing.
- A: The skill frames the safe sidecar syntax as the *intersection* of Arc 2d and full Mermaid, so a
  diagram authored for a sidecar also renders correctly on GitHub — which is the rule to follow when
  it is unclear where a diagram will be read.

#### D3 - Move the test's content anchors off the guidance being removed

- D: `test_extracted_lazy_skills_are_registered_seeded_and_standalone` required the literal phrase
  "invisible `~~~` links"; it now requires "`subgraph` Is Not Parsed In Sidecars" and "is the only
  arrow that works".
- R: The test pins representative phrases so a skill cannot be gutted silently. It was pinning the
  exact sentence this change deletes, so it failed by design and the fix is to re-anchor it, not to
  weaken it. The new anchors were chosen to pin the two claims that carry the change — the
  prohibition and the single-arrow rule — so a future edit that quietly reinstates subgraphs trips it.
- A: Left `memory-system-version: 2.19` alone; nothing required a bump and it would be a delta the
  brief did not ask for.
